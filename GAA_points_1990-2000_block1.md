# GAA Senior Football Championship — Alternative Points Analysis
## Block 1: 1990–2000 (the pre-qualifier, no-quarter-final era)

**Points system**
- 5 — All-Ireland champion
- 4 — All-Ireland runner-up
- 3 — losing All-Ireland semi-finalist (×2)
- 2 — losing All-Ireland quarter-finalist (×4) — *does not exist in this era*
- 1 — beaten provincial finalist (×4)

**Method.** From 1990–2000 the four provincial champions went straight to the All-Ireland
semi-finals; there was no quarter-final round, so no team scores 2. In every one of these
years both All-Ireland finalists were provincial champions, so the two losing semi-finalists
are simply the two provincial champions who did not reach the final. Scoring is by best finish
only. The four beaten provincial finalists each score 1.

Checksum: 5 + 4 + 3 + 3 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 = **19 points/year**, ×11 years = **209**.
