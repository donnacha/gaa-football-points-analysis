# GAA Senior Football Championship — Alternative Points Analysis
## Block 2: 2001–2017 (the qualifier / "back-door" era)

**Points system (full ladder applies)**
- 5 — All-Ireland champion
- 4 — All-Ireland runner-up
- 3 — losing All-Ireland semi-finalist (×2)
- 2 — losing All-Ireland quarter-finalist (×4)
- 1 — beaten provincial finalist whose run **ended at the provincial final**

**Method & the key subtlety.** From 2001 the back door fed an 8-team All-Ireland
quarter-final (4 provincial champions + 4 qualifier-route teams). Scoring is **best finish
only**, which matters a lot here: beaten provincial finalists re-entered via the qualifiers
and frequently went deep. A beaten provincial finalist who reaches the QF scores 2, a semi
3, the final 4 — and in 2005 Tyrone *won the All-Ireland* as a beaten Ulster finalist (5).
So the 1-point tier in any year is **only** those beaten provincial finalists eliminated at
the provincial-final stage. The fixed top-8 slots always sum to 23 (5+4+3+3+2+2+2+2); the
yearly total is 23 + (number of beaten provincial finalists who did not reach the QF), i.e.
between 23 and 27.

Note also: the 2-/3-/4-/5-point tiers can include teams that were **not** provincial
finalists at all (qualifier teams that lost earlier in their province but ran deep).

## Year-by-year point assignments

**2001** — Champion Galway. Meath (beaten Leinster finalist) ran to the final.
- 5 Galway · 4 Meath · 3 Derry, Kerry · 2 Roscommon, Dublin, Tyrone, Westmeath · 1 Mayo, Cork, Cavan  *(=26)*

**2002** — Armagh's first title.
- 5 Armagh · 4 Kerry · 3 Cork, Dublin · 2 Galway, Sligo, Mayo, Donegal · 1 Kildare, Tipperary  *(=25)*

**2003** — Tyrone's first title; all-Ulster final.
- 5 Tyrone · 4 Armagh · 3 Kerry, Donegal · 2 Fermanagh, Laois, Galway, Roscommon · 1 Mayo, Kildare, Limerick, Down  *(=27)*

**2004** — Kerry champions.
- 5 Kerry · 4 Mayo · 3 Fermanagh, Derry · 2 Armagh, Tyrone, Westmeath, Dublin · 1 Roscommon, Laois, Limerick, Donegal  *(=27)*

**2005** — Tyrone win as **beaten Ulster finalists** (back-door champions). All four beaten
provincial finalists went deeper than the provincial final, so nobody scores 1.
- 5 Tyrone · 4 Kerry · 3 Cork, Armagh · 2 Mayo, Galway, Dublin, Laois  *(=23)*

**2006** — Kerry win as beaten Munster finalists.
- 5 Kerry · 4 Mayo · 3 Cork, Dublin · 2 Armagh, Donegal, Westmeath, Laois · 1 Galway, Offaly  *(=25)*

**2007** — Kerry champions; Cork reach final as beaten Munster finalists.
- 5 Kerry · 4 Cork · 3 Meath, Dublin · 2 Sligo, Tyrone, Derry, Monaghan · 1 Galway, Laois  *(=25)*

**2008** — Tyrone champions; Kerry reach final as beaten Munster finalists.
- 5 Tyrone · 4 Kerry · 3 Cork, Wexford · 2 Armagh, Galway, Kildare, Dublin · 1 Mayo, Fermanagh  *(=25)*

**2009** — Kerry champions.
- 5 Kerry · 4 Cork · 3 Tyrone, Meath · 2 Donegal, Kildare, Dublin, Mayo · 1 Galway, Limerick, Antrim  *(=26)*

**2010** — Cork champions.
- 5 Cork · 4 Down · 3 Dublin, Kildare · 2 Kerry, Tyrone, Roscommon, Meath · 1 Sligo, Louth, Limerick, Monaghan  *(=27)*

**2011** — Dublin's first of the modern run.
- 5 Dublin · 4 Kerry · 3 Mayo, Donegal · 2 Limerick, Cork, Kildare, Tyrone · 1 Roscommon, Wexford, Derry  *(=26)*

**2012** — Donegal champions.
- 5 Donegal · 4 Mayo · 3 Cork, Dublin · 2 Down, Laois, Kildare, Kerry · 1 Sligo, Meath, Clare  *(=26)*

**2013** — Dublin champions.
- 5 Dublin · 4 Mayo · 3 Tyrone, Kerry · 2 Cork, Cavan, Donegal, Monaghan · 1 London, Meath  *(=25)*

**2014** — Kerry champions; three of four beaten provincial finalists reached the QF.
- 5 Kerry · 4 Donegal · 3 Mayo, Dublin · 2 Galway, Cork, Armagh, Monaghan · 1 Meath  *(=24)*

**2015** — Dublin champions.
- 5 Dublin · 4 Kerry · 3 Tyrone, Mayo · 2 Kildare, Fermanagh, Monaghan, Donegal · 1 Sligo, Westmeath, Cork  *(=26)*

**2016** — Dublin champions; Tipperary's run to the semi as a non-finalist is the eye-catcher.
- 5 Dublin · 4 Mayo · 3 Tipperary, Kerry · 2 Clare, Galway, Tyrone, Donegal · 1 Roscommon, Westmeath  *(=25)*

**2017** — Dublin champions.
- 5 Dublin · 4 Mayo · 3 Kerry, Tyrone · 2 Galway, Roscommon, Armagh, Monaghan · 1 Leitrim, Kildare, Cork, Down  *(=27)*

Sum of yearly totals: **435** (cross-checks against the per-county totals below).
