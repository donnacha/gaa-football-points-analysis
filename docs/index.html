The idea

A single All-Ireland is worth a lot — but it tells you little about the counties who were perennially close. This system scores every county on how far it got each year, so consistency across 138 championships (1887–2025) becomes visible.

Why averages matter. The championship changed shape repeatedly over 138 years (see By era), so raw totals reward longevity. The Avg / scoring year column in the standings is the era-fair view: how deep a county runs when it shows up. The new Roll of honour, Finals, League & doubles and Records tabs query the same database from other angles.

Methodology

Each county scores its best finish only in a given year (a champion scores 5, not 5 plus extra for also winning its province). Points:

PointsFinishing position
5All-Ireland champion
4All-Ireland runner-up
3Beaten All-Ireland semi-finalist
2Beaten All-Ireland quarter-finalist (incl. Super 8s group exit, 2018–19)
1Provincial finalist (winner or runner-up), or All-Ireland group-stage / preliminary-QF exit (2023–25)

Handling the changing formats

The structure changed repeatedly across 138 years, so the tiers map differently by era:

  • 1887–1927 — foundation era. An irregular early knockout: provincial champions (with London, later New York, sometimes contesting the final) met in semi-finals and a final. Beaten semi-finalists score 3; the rare extra early rounds map to the 2-point tier.
  • 1928–1989 — Sam Maguire knockout. The settled four-province knockout — four provincial champions into two semi-finals and a final. No quarter-final, so the two beaten semi-finalists take the 3-point tier and there are no 2s.
  • 1990–2000 — pre-qualifier. Four provincial champions went straight to the semi-finals; there was no quarter-final, so nobody scores 2.
  • 2001–2017 & 2022 — qualifier / back-door. An 8-team knockout quarter-final; the full ladder applies. Beaten provincial finalists who went deep via the qualifiers score their deeper finish (best-finish rule).
  • 2018–2019 — Super 8s. The quarter-final round was a group of eight; the four teams eliminated at that stage take the 2-point tier.
  • 2020–2021 — COVID knockout. Straight knockout, four provincial champions into the semi-finals — structurally identical to the early 1990s, no quarter-final.
  • 2023–2025 — round-robin. A 16-team group stage feeds preliminary quarter-finals and quarter-finals. Reaching the group stage but exiting before the quarter-finals earns 1 point.
Reproducible. The site is generated from a small open database: raw results in championship.json, an editable scoring table in params.json, and build.py, which applies the best-finish rule. Change a parameter, re-run, and every table here updates. See the repository.
On the historical record. For the settled era (1928–1989) the two beaten semi-finalists are derived as the provincial champions who didn't reach the final and cross-checked automatically; the irregular foundation era (1887–1927) was researched year-by-year. Champion, runner-up and provincial-title counts reconcile exactly with the canonical totals (Kerry 39, Dublin 31, Galway 9…). A handful of beaten-finalist names in the sparsest early years carry mild uncertainty and are flagged in the source files; they are a tiny share of points and don't affect the modern standings.

Sources: per-year All-Ireland and provincial championship records, the lists of All-Ireland finals, winning captains and managers, and National League finals (Wikipedia), cross-checked round by round. Final scores, venues, attendances, captains and managers are stored alongside the results in the database.
