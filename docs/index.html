The idea

A single All-Ireland is worth a lot — but it tells you little about the counties who were perennially close. This system scores every county on how far it got each year, so consistency across 138 championships (1887–2025) becomes visible.

Methodology

Each county scores its best finish only in a given year (a champion scores 5, not 5 plus extra for also winning its province). Points:

PointsFinishing position
5All-Ireland champion
4All-Ireland runner-up
3Beaten All-Ireland semi-finalist
2Beaten All-Ireland quarter-finalist — or, in the no-quarter-final knockout eras (1928–2000 and 2020–21), a beaten provincial finalist (Super 8s group exit, 2018–19, also scores here)
1Beaten provincial semi-finalist in the no-quarter-final eras; or All-Ireland group-stage / preliminary-QF exit (2023–25); or a beaten provincial finalist in eras that have a real All-Ireland quarter-final

In the pure-knockout eras the four provincial champions went straight to the All-Ireland semi-finals, so the championship had no real quarter-final — but the provincial rounds are the lower rounds of the same bracket. By default the analysis credits a beaten provincial finalist as a de-facto quarter-finalist (the 2s) and a beaten provincial semi-finalist as a de-facto preliminary-round loser (the 1s). This is the provincial_bracket_credit switch in params.json (on by default); turning it off reverts to scoring beaten provincial finalists at 1 and leaving provincial semi-finalists unscored.

Handling the changing formats

The structure changed repeatedly across 138 years, so the tiers map differently by era:

  • 1887–1927 — foundation era. An irregular early knockout: provincial champions (with London, later New York, sometimes contesting the final) met in semi-finals and a final. Beaten semi-finalists score 3; the rare extra early rounds map to the 2-point tier.
  • 1928–1989 — Sam Maguire knockout. The settled four-province knockout — four provincial champions into two semi-finals and a final. The two beaten All-Ireland semi-finalists take the 3-point tier. There was no real quarter-final, so the provincial final stands in as one: beaten provincial finalists score 2 and beaten provincial semi-finalists score 1 (the provincial_bracket_credit default).
  • 1990–2000 — pre-qualifier. Four provincial champions went straight to the semi-finals — structurally identical to the Sam Maguire era, so the same mapping applies: beaten provincial finalists score 2, beaten provincial semi-finalists score 1.
  • 2001–2017 & 2022 — qualifier / back-door. An 8-team knockout quarter-final; the full ladder applies. Beaten provincial finalists who went deep via the qualifiers score their deeper finish (best-finish rule).
  • 2018–2019 — Super 8s. The quarter-final round was a group of eight; the four teams eliminated at that stage take the 2-point tier.
  • 2020–2021 — COVID knockout. Straight knockout, four provincial champions into the semi-finals — structurally identical to the early 1990s, no quarter-final, so the same credit applies: beaten provincial finalists score 2, beaten provincial semi-finalists score 1.
  • 2023–2025 — round-robin. A 16-team group stage feeds preliminary quarter-finals and quarter-finals. Reaching the group stage but exiting before the quarter-finals earns 1 point.

Adding the National League

The standings table's League and Combined columns fold in the National League. Because the league and the championship are separate competitions run in different parts of the year, their points are added within a season rather than taking the best of the two (a county can genuinely win the league and reach the All-Ireland final in the same year). The league sits below the championship — a Division 1 title scores 2 (a championship quarter-final), a league final 1, and a Division 1 semi-final or any lower-division title 0.5 — set under league_points in params.json. The league dates from 1926, so earlier seasons are championship-only. The Year-by-year tab can fold the same league points into the heatmap with the Add league points toggle.

Reproducible. The site is generated from a small open database: raw results in championship.json, an editable scoring table in params.json, and build.py, which applies the best-finish rule. Change a parameter, re-run, and every table here updates. See the repository.
On the historical record. For the settled era (1928–1989) the two beaten semi-finalists are derived as the provincial champions who didn't reach the final and cross-checked automatically; the irregular foundation era (1887–1927) was researched year-by-year. Champion, runner-up and provincial-title counts reconcile exactly with the canonical totals (Kerry 39, Dublin 31, Galway 9…). A handful of beaten-finalist names in the sparsest early years carry mild uncertainty and are flagged in the source files; they are a tiny share of points and don't affect the modern standings.

Sources: per-year All-Ireland and provincial championship records, the lists of All-Ireland finals, winning captains and managers, and National League finals (Wikipedia), cross-checked round by round.
