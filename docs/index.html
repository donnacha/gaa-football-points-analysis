The idea

Winning an All-Ireland is worth a lot. It just doesn't tell you much about the counties who were always in it and never quite got over the line. So this scores every county on how far it got, every year, across 138 championships from 1887 on. Consistency stops being invisible.

Methodology

Each county only scores its best finish in a year. Win the All-Ireland and you get 5, not 5 plus a bit extra for the province you also won on the way. The tiers:

PointsFinishing position
5All-Ireland champion
4All-Ireland runner-up
3Beaten All-Ireland semi-finalist
2Beaten All-Ireland quarter-finalist. In the no-quarter-final knockout eras (1928 to 2000, and 2020 to 21) a beaten provincial finalist scores here. Super 8s group exits (2018 to 19) land here too.
1Beaten provincial semi-finalist in those no-quarter-final eras; or an All-Ireland group-stage or preliminary-QF exit (2023 to 25); or a beaten provincial finalist in the eras that do have a real All-Ireland quarter-final.

In the pure-knockout eras the four provincial champions went straight into the All-Ireland semi-finals, so there was no real quarter-final at all. But the provincial rounds are the lower rounds of the same bracket, really. So by default a beaten provincial finalist gets credited as a stand-in quarter-finalist (the 2s) and a beaten provincial semi-finalist as a stand-in preliminary-round loser (the 1s). That's the provincial_bracket_credit switch in params.json, on by default. Turn it off and beaten provincial finalists drop to 1, with provincial semi-finalists getting nothing.

A changeable format

The format has been torn up and rebuilt over and over across 138 years, so the tiers land differently depending on the era:

  • 1887–1927, foundation era. A messy, irregular early knockout.
  • 1928–1989, Sam Maguire knockout. The settled four-province format: four provincial champions, two semi-finals, a final. The two beaten All-Ireland semi-finalists take 3. No real quarter-final here, so the provincial final stands in for one. Beaten provincial finalists get 2, beaten provincial semi-finalists 1.
  • 1990–2000, pre-qualifier. Same shape as the Sam Maguire era, four champions straight into the semis. Same mapping: provincial finalists 2, provincial semi-finalists 1.
  • 2001–2017 and 2022, qualifier / back-door. A proper 8-team quarter-final, so the full ladder applies. Provincial finalists who went on a qualifier run score their deeper finish, not the province.
  • 2018–2019, Super 8s. The quarter-final became a group of eight. The four knocked out at that stage take 2.
  • 2020–2021, COVID knockout. Straight knockout again, four champions into the semis, just like the early 90s. Same credit: provincial finalists 2, provincial semi-finalists 1.
  • 2023–2025, round-robin. A 16-team group stage into preliminary quarter-finals and quarter-finals. Make the groups but go out before the quarters and you get 1.

Adding the National League

The standings table's League and Combined columns add the National League on top. It sits below the championship: a Division 1 title is 2, a league final 1, a Division 1 semi-final or any lower-division title 0.5. All of that is set in league_points in params.json. The league only goes back to 1926, so anything earlier is championship-only. The Year-by-year tab can fold the same points into the heatmap with the Add league points toggle.

Reproducible. The whole site is built from a small open database: the raw results in championship.json, a scoring table you can edit in params.json, and build.py to apply the best-finish rule. Change a number, re-run, and every table here moves. It's all in the repository.
On the historical record. For the settled era (1928 to 1989) the two beaten semi-finalists are worked out as the provincial champions who didn't reach the final, then cross-checked automatically. The messy foundation era (1887 to 1927) was dug out year by year. Champion, runner-up and provincial-title counts line up exactly with the canonical totals (Kerry 39, Dublin 31, Galway 9, and so on). A few beaten-finalist names in the sparsest early years are still a bit uncertain, and flagged in the source files. They're a tiny share of the points and don't touch the modern standings.
